Guidelines & Steps to Participate

  1. Work must be original. Plagiarism or purely re-posted (self-plagiarism) content without significant modification is strictly not allowed. If you choose to resubmit one of your previous blog or article contents, please ensure that it is enhanced with new insights, updates or additional value to make it distinct!
  2. Avoid AI-generated content (taking help of AI tools to improve the grammar or style is fine, however make sure it does NOT hallucinate) - let your own insights shine!
  3. Language: English only.
  4. Submission format: PDF only, text-selectable (should not be flattened). Times New Roman font with size 12px suggested.
  5. One submission per student/team
  6. Max 2 pages of main content excluding bib. Cite references as needed.
  7. Submissions are reviewed by the initiative core team (novelty, creativity are some of the many filtering checks); decisions are final.
  8. Shortlisted articles will be featured in the Wayanad e-newsletter (with participant credits & certificates for all entries).

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I submit an article as a team?

Yes! Collaborative articles are encouraged, especially if you participated as a team in previous ML contests.

Can I update or revise my article after initial submission?

Yes! Please check our portal guide.

Are code snippets or diagrams allowed?

Yes, as long as they are relevant and clearly presented, and you cite any sources if not original.

Do I get a certificate for participation?

Yes! Every submitted article (meeting guidelines) earns a digital certificate from Wayanad House.

How will I be notified if my article is selected?

You’ll get an email at your registered address once selections are announced.
